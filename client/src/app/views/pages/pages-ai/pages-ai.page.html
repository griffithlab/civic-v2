CIViC MCP Server

The Clinical Interpretation of Variants in Cancer (CIViC) knowledgebase provides a community-driven, open-source platform for discussing the biological and Clinical Significance of molecular variants in cancer. To enable users to make complex connections between CIViC information, we developed the CIViC Model Context Protocol (MCP) server, which allows large language models (LLMs) to directly interface with the CIViC API through natural language, facilitating the rapid summarization of expertly curated cancer variant interpretations.

Repository: CIViC-Model-Context-Protocol/civic-mcp-server
Preprint: CIViC MCP: Integrating Large Language Models with the Clinical Interpretations of Variants in Cancer

CIViC-Fact

Accurate interpretation of genomic variants is critical for precision oncology but remains slow and dependent on specialized expertise. Public knowledgebases such as the Clinical Interpretation of Variants in Cancer (CIViC) help by curating literature-backed variant interpretations in a structured form, yet verification and review have become major bottlenecks. To address this, we developed CIViC-Fact, a benchmark dataset and pipeline for testing automated systems that verify the accuracy of cancer variant claims. CIViC-Fact links structured claims to sentence-level supporting or refuting evidence from full-text articles, and includes expert annotations and explanations.

Repository: creisle/civicfact
Preprint: Evaluating Language Models for Biomedical Fact-Checking: A Benchmark Dataset for Cancer Variant Interpretation Verification

CIViCmine

To aid curation and provide the greatest coverage for precision oncology databases, particularly CIViC, we propose the use of text mining approaches to extract these clinically relevant biomarkers from all available published literature. To this end, a group of cancer genomics experts annotated sentences that discussed biomarkers with their clinical associations and achieved good inter-annotator agreement. We then used a supervised learning approach to construct the CIViCmine knowledgebase. We extracted 121,589 relevant sentences from PubMed abstracts and PubMed Central Open Access full-text papers. CIViCmine contains over 87,412 biomarkers associated with 8035 genes, 337 drugs, and 572 cancer types, representing 25,818 abstracts and 39,795 full-text publications. Through integration with CIVIC, we provide a prioritized list of curatable clinically relevant cancer biomarkers as well as a resource that is valuable to other knowledgebases and precision cancer analysts in general. All data is publicly available and distributed with a Creative Commons Zero license.

Website: http://bionlp.bcgsc.ca/civicmine/
Repository: jakelever/civicmine
Publication: Text-mining clinically relevant cancer biomarkers for curation into the CIViC database

CIViC Natural Language Inference (CIViC NLI)

The CIViC natural language interpretation (NLI) project will create a fact-checking companion data set for CIViC. The aim of this project is to annotate relevant sections of the full-text of articles that are used to support evidence items in the Clinical Interpretation of Variants in Cancer database (CIViC). This will create a novel high-quality oncology-focused data set for natural language inference (NLI). he particular sentences/spans in the text which should be selected are those that are sufficient and required to ensure the article does indeed contain/support the CIViC entry.

Repository: creisle/civic-nli-ext
Extension: CIViC-NLI on the Chrome Web Store
Documentation: Getting Started with CIViC NLI

CIViC AI Editorial Policies

CIViC embraces innovation to enhance curation efficiency while maintaining our foundation as a trustworthy, human-curated resource. These policies govern how AI tools may be used to support—but never replace—expert judgment. We allow AI for supportive tasks when accompanied by strict verification, ensuring that CIViC remains an accurate and accountable resource for the precision medicine community.

Editorial Goals

  • Maintain the integrity and reliability of CIViC’s content
  • Encourage responsible use of AI
  • Protect the collaborative and educational spirit of CIViC
  • Provide clarity
  • Ensure transparency

Allowed Uses of AI

AI tools are permitted as supportive assistants to save time and facilitate discovery, provided that a human remains in full control of the final output.

  • Language and Grammar Assistance
  • Summarizing Published Literature
  • Idea Generation and Research Aid
  • Formatting, Tagging, and Data Handling
  • Internal QA and Detection

Prohibited Uses of AI

  • No Fully AI-Generated Submissions
  • No AI-Only Interpretations or Conclusions
  • No Undisclosed AI Co-production
  • No AI-Generated References or Data
  • No Automated Bulk Content Creation
  • No Confidential or Proprietary Data in AI Queries

Verification and Compliance

Contributors bear full responsibility for thorough verification of all AI-assisted content to ensure scientific accuracy and tone. The complete policy details enforcement measures for violations, monitoring strategies, and provides concrete examples of compliant versus non-compliant workflows.
